The folder you open in VS Code is the world Claude can see. Here's where everything lives so you never have to hunt — including the three folders you'll actually touch.

The shape of a lesson's folder

# inside your workspace (the "2026-27 Activity Creation" shared drive) module_plans/ ← the blueprint (planning sheets), by curriculum & grade source_material/ ← the raw source texts draft_outputs/ {Curriculum}/{DATE}_{Lesson_ID}/ {Lesson_ID}_Activity_Content.md ← YOU edit this {Lesson_ID}_Match_Report.md ← read-only: Claude's reasoning _DO_NOT_EDIT/ ← the untouched original (never edit) _pipeline/ ← system metadata (ignore) review_ready/ ← your draft lands here after /move-to-review final_outputs/ ← approved & finalized activities

Two folders to leave alone. Never edit anything in _DO_NOT_EDIT/ or _pipeline/. The first is what lets the system show what you changed; the second is internal bookkeeping.

The journey of one activity

  1. Module plan savedYou fill in the blueprint in module_plans/.
  2. Ticket created & claimedA Jira ticket = a lesson, pointing at that plan row.
  3. You drag the ticket to "Draft Activity with Claude"That drop is the trigger — the pipeline picks it up.
  4. Claude builds the draftIt appears in draft_outputs/… with the Activity Content, Match Report, and backup. Most builds finish overnight — drop your tickets and check in the morning.
  5. Ticket auto-moves to "Draft Review"Now you review and edit Activity_Content.md.
  6. You run /move-to-reviewYour draft moves to review_ready/ and the lead is notified.
  7. Lead reviews & finalizesApproved work lands in final_outputs/ for the transformation team.
Want the exact folder for your lesson? Ask Mojo "where's the draft for TX_BBO_XX_G5_1.0_7?" and it'll point you right to it.
