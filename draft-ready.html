Is my draft ready to submit?

A quick self-check before you run /move-to-review

← Guide Home FAQ

Run through this before you submit. You don't need every box perfect — but the more that are, the smoother the lead review. (Drawn from the Reviewer Guide and the draft-quality rubric.)

Alignment & coverage

Does the draft do what the plan asked?

  • The activity matches the objective from your module plan.
  • Every driving question and analytical move the plan called for is actually in the draft.
  • Anything the plan said to include verbatim appears word-for-word — not paraphrased.

Question sequence & scaffolding

Do the questions build to the assessment?

  • Questions build logically: accessible entry → comprehension → analysis → assessment.
  • Each question is right for the grade — not too obvious, not over-reaching.
  • Adjacent questions don't double-test the same detail or skill.

Text grounding

Is everything anchored to this text?

  • Paragraph references are accurate to the excerpt provided.
  • If a question names a paragraph, its criterion references the same paragraph(s).
  • Criteria use this text's actual names, details, and quotes — not generic, swap-in-any-text language.

Criteria quality

Each non-MC question needs a criterion that mirrors the question.

  • Every non-MC question has a criterion.
  • Generated criteria (LCQ, TDQ, Highlight, Claim, Brainstorm) stay to one sentence.
  • No "sneak-adds": the criterion asks for exactly what the stem asks — no extra requirements or invented counts.
  • "Or" joins real alternatives; "and" only when the question explicitly asks for two separate moves.
  • Vocabulary is grade-appropriate.

Assessment (the Target Task)

Does it land the analytical move?

  • The Claim connects logically to the driving questions and flows from the sequence.
  • The write/MC task is clear and appropriately scoped for the grade.
  • The driving questions and Target Task work together as a coherent whole.

Content blocks

Intro, Transition, Conclusion — without spoilers.

  • The Introduction sets a focus without giving away the answer or the Target Task's conclusion.
  • The Transition signals the shift to the write, names the concept and the text, but doesn't preview the actual question.
  • The Conclusion stamps the generalizable skill (not just what happened in this text) and congratulates students.

Grade fit & tone

  • Vocabulary, sentence complexity, and evidence demands match the grade.
  • Tone is warm and accessible — not performative or over-hyped.

⚠ Red flags — stop & fix before submitting

  • A non-MC question with no criterion.
  • Generated criteria longer than one sentence.
  • Paragraph references that don't match the excerpt.
  • Verbatim plan content that got paraphrased or reworded.
  • Generic criteria that could apply to any text.
  • An Introduction that states the theme or gives away the Target Task.
  • A Transition that repeats or previews the assessment question.
  • Grade-level vocabulary that's clearly too high or too low.
Not sure about one of these? Ask Mojo — e.g. "what makes a good criterion?" or "what's the rule for the Transition block?" — and it'll pull the exact guidance.
Genuinely unsure if it's good enough? That's a judgment call — submit it and let your lead weigh in, or use "Ask the team." Better to ask than to guess.
