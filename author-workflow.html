Complete Your Module Plan
Before anything runs, the plan must be in place
You

Your module plan is the blueprint that drives everything downstream. Each row represents one activity. Claude reads this plan to determine what to build.

What You Need to Fill In

  • Activity ID — the unique identifier that tracks this activity through the entire pipeline
  • Activity Objective — what students will learn or do
  • Anchor Text — the source text for this lesson
  • Exact Text Excerpt — the specific section of the text (e.g., "Ch. 6" or "paragraphs 58–120"). When filled in, Claude goes directly to this section.
  • Text Segment — broader reading range with guidance (e.g., "Excerpts from Chs. 1–3; 700–900 words; focus on the idea of being 'ordinary' vs 'extraordinary'"). Claude selects the best excerpt within this range.
  • Target Task (Verbatim) — the assessment question, word-for-word from the curriculum
  • Driving Questions (Verbatim & Outline) — scaffolding questions and guidance
  • Planning Notes — how closely Claude should follow the original curriculum

Keep it lean. Give Claude direction, not copy-pasted content. Reference where texts live — Claude goes and pulls them. The leaner your plan, the fewer transcription errors and the more scalable the process.

Where to Save It

Save your completed module plan CSV to the Curriculum Hub inside of 00. Curriculum & Text - Raw Materials, under the correct curriculum and grade folder.

2
Confirm Source Materials Are Ready
Verify everything Claude needs is in the Curriculum Hub
You

Before you move any ticket, check that the source texts your plan references actually exist in the Curriculum Hub folders.

Pre-Flight Checklist

  • Every text referenced in your plan exists in the correct Hub folder
  • Supplemental materials (handouts, tool pages) are in place if referenced
  • For novel/supplemental curricula: if you sourced a text yourself (e.g., a TED talk transcript), it's uploaded to the Hub
  • Your Activity IDs are present in the module plan

If source materials are missing, the build will fail. Claude will not fabricate content. The ticket will stall in Drafting and show an error. Better to catch this now than after the build runs.

What Happens Behind the Scenes

The pipeline polls Jira every 30 minutes during business hours. When it finds your ticket in "Drafting," it:

  1. Tags the ticket as pipeline_queued so it doesn't get picked up twice
  2. Reads your module plan row for this Activity ID
  3. Runs the three-skill build sequence: Populate → Pick Excerpt → Draft Activity
  4. Runs the 11-point quality check
  5. If successful, auto-moves the ticket to Draft Review

5
Claude Builds Your Draft
Automated — you wait for this to complete
Automated

While you wait (usually within the next polling cycle), Claude runs three skills in sequence:

SkillWhat It DoesTime
/populate-lesson Navigates the Curriculum Hub, copies source texts and lesson plans to the working folder ~3–5 min
/pick-excerpt Selects and formats the text excerpt with correct paragraph numbering ~3–5 min
/draft-activity Generates the full activity: intro, questions, criteria, assessment, conclusion ~8–12 min
/edit-draft Runs 11-point quality validation and auto-fixes issues ~5–8 min

Total build time: approximately 20–30 minutes. Times vary depending on text length and complexity. Don't worry if it takes a while — the pipeline will move your ticket automatically when it's done.

Review Your Draft
Read the output and assess it against the plan
You

Find Your Draft

Open VS Code. Navigate to draft_outputs/ on the shared drive. Your draft folder is named with the date and Activity ID:

Your File

draft_outputs/2026-04-01_OE_SLP_XX_G9_1.1_7_v1/

Open this: _Activity_Content.md — this is the draft you'll review and edit
Reference: _Match_Report.md — explains why Claude made the choices it did
Don't touch: _DO_NOT_EDIT/ — backup files for change tracking

First Read: Top to Bottom

Read the entire draft without editing. Get a feel for the arc. Then check:

  • Does the activity match the objective from your module plan?
  • Do the driving questions scaffold logically toward the target task?
  • Are paragraph references accurate to the actual excerpt?
  • Are verbatim questions unchanged from what you wrote in the plan?
  • Do the criteria and example responses feel realistic for this grade level?
  • Is the tone right for students?

The Match Report is your friend. If a question or design choice surprises you, check the _Match_Report.md — it documents which exemplars Claude referenced and why it made specific decisions.

7
Edit & Refine
Make the draft yours — three ways to edit
You

Option A: Edit Directly

For small changes — typos, rewording, adjusting criteria — just edit the markdown file in VS Code and save.

Option B: Ask Claude

For larger structural changes, describe what you want in the Claude panel. Be specific:

Example Prompts

"Question 3 feels too easy — make it more analytically demanding while keeping the same text focus."

"The success criteria for Question 5 are too vague — tighten them with specific paragraph references."

"Swap Questions 2 and 3 — students need the vocabulary context before tackling inference."

"The excerpt is too long — trim it to focus on paragraphs 14–22."

"Add a highlight question before Question 4 to scaffold the close reading."

Claude makes the edits in the file and explains what changed. You can review, approve, or ask for further adjustments.

Option C: Run /edit-draft

For a comprehensive quality pass, type /edit-draft in the Claude panel. This re-runs the 11-point validation and auto-fixes what it can. Use this if you've made significant changes and want a fresh check.

You can't break anything. The _DO_NOT_EDIT/ folder has a backup of the original draft. If something goes sideways, Claude can restore from there.

Error States & Stalled Tickets
What to do when something doesn't go right

How to Spot a Problem

If your ticket has been in Drafting for more than one polling cycle (~30 minutes) and hasn't moved to Draft Review, the build may have stalled.

Common Causes & Fixes

Missing Source Text

Claude couldn't find the text referenced in your plan. Fix: Check the Curriculum Hub for the file. Upload it if missing, then drag the ticket back to Drafting to re-trigger.

Missing Activity ID

The module plan row doesn't have an Activity ID. Fix: Add the ID to your plan, save, and re-trigger the ticket.

Module Plan Not Found

The plan CSV isn't in the expected folder or doesn't match the naming convention. Fix: Verify the file is in module_plans/{Curriculum}/{Grade}/ and re-trigger.

Ambiguous Excerpt Reference

The Text Segment was too vague for Claude to identify the right passage. Fix: Add more specificity to the Exact Text Excerpt or Text Segment column (e.g., page numbers, paragraph ranges).

Supplemental Text Missing

"Requires Supplemental Text" is marked Yes but the supplemental file isn't in the Hub. Fix: Upload the supplemental material and re-trigger.

Polling Window Missed

You moved the ticket outside business hours or between polling cycles. Fix: Just wait — the next polling cycle will pick it up. The pipeline runs every 30 minutes during business hours.

How to Re-Trigger a Stalled Ticket

  1. Fix the underlying issue (upload missing file, update plan, etc.)
  2. Drag the ticket back to Backlog or Ready for Authoring
  3. Drag it to Drafting again
  4. The pipeline will pick it up on the next cycle

If you're stuck: Reach out to Michaela. She can check the pipeline logs, diagnose what went wrong, and help resolve it quickly. Don't spend time guessing — that's what she's here for.
