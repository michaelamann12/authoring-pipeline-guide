Quick answers to the most common questions. Don't see yours? Ask Mojo, or use "Ask the team."

Getting a draft built
How long does a build take?

You don't wait on it. The model to expect is overnight: drop your tickets into "Draft Activity with Claude" during the day, and most will be drafted by the next morning. (Each lesson takes roughly 45–60 minutes of build time, but that runs in the background — you're not part of the run.)

How do I start a build?

Drag the ticket into the "Draft Activity with Claude" column on the Jira board. That drop is the trigger — the pipeline picks it up automatically. No other tool needed.

I dragged my ticket but nothing happened — no draft appeared.

A few things to check before re-dragging:

1) The ticket's Activity ID must match a row in the module plan — a mismatch means the pipeline can't find what to build. 2) The source materials the plan references need to be in place. 3) Builds run on a cycle and most complete overnight, so give it time. Don't re-drag repeatedly — that can create duplicate builds. If it's still empty the next day, use "Ask the team."

Finding & editing your work
Where do I find my draft?

In draft_outputs/{Curriculum}/{DATE}_{Lesson_ID}/ — the file you edit is {Lesson_ID}_Activity_Content.md. Full map on the Where do I find…? page.

Should I edit by hand or ask Claude?

Edit by hand for small, known fixes — a typo, a word swap, a quick deletion. Ask Claude for bigger or repetitive work — rewriting a question, reworking criteria, "make Q3 harder," "swap Q2 and Q3." Both are fine; use whichever is faster for the change.

What's the Match Report for?

It's Claude's reasoning for the draft — which exemplars it used and why it built the questions the way it did. It's read-only; read it to understand the draft before you edit. You only edit the Activity_Content.md file.

Can I edit files in the _DO_NOT_EDIT folder?

No. That folder holds the untouched original of the draft — it's what lets the system show exactly what you changed when you submit. Leave it alone (and ignore _pipeline/ too).

What's the difference between an LCQ and a TDQ?

An LCQ (Literal Comprehension) asks what the text says — the answer is right there. A TDQ (Text-Dependent) asks what it means or how you know — grounded in the text but takes inference. More terms on the Glossary.

Submitting & the board
How do I submit my draft for review?

Run /move-to-review. It writes a summary of your changes, moves the draft to review_ready/, advances the ticket to Needs Lead Review, and notifies your lead. You don't drag the ticket yourself for this step.

What are the board statuses, in order?

Backlog → (Authoring — optional prep) → Draft Activity with Claude (you drop it here to build) → Draft Review (you edit) → Needs Lead Review (after /move-to-review) → Final Review / In Transformation (lead finalizes) → Done.

When to ask a person
I think the source text has an error, or the standard doesn't fit the passage.

That's a judgment call, not something to fix on your own — use "Ask the team." Flag what you're seeing and let a lead decide.

Is my draft good enough to finalize?

That's the lead's call. Run the Is my draft ready? self-check, submit with /move-to-review, and your lead will tell you if anything needs another pass. When in doubt, ask rather than guess.

Still stuck? Ask Mojo in the panel — it reads the guide and the reference docs and will point you to the exact answer, or hand you to the team when it's a judgment call.
